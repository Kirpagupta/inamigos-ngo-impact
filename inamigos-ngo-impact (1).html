Who We Are

Founded on the belief that collective action outlasts individual charity.

InAmigos Foundation was founded on 23 September 2020 by Mr. Govind Shukla, with a vision to build a kinder, more equal society. What began as a small volunteer effort in Bilaspur, Chhattisgarh has grown into a Section 8 registered non-profit working across 28 states, guided by 80G & 12A tax certifications, CSR-1 registration, NITI Aayog listing, and ISO 9001:2015 quality standards.

We believe a charity's work should centre on those society overlooks most easily: children without access to schooling, women without financial independence, animals without protection, and the environment without a voice. Every rupee and every volunteer hour is directed toward closing one of these gaps.

Our internship programme, Project Vikas, has trained more than 30,000 young people in professional and digital skills — and over 250 of them have gone on to join the foundation permanently. It's how "Uniting Minds for a Change" moves from a tagline to a working model.

The gaps we work to close

  • Lack of education access caused by poverty
  • Loss of green cover from unchecked deforestation
  • Limited financial independence for women
  • Little to no protection for stray and injured animals
  • Declining environmental quality in underserved regions
  • Few structured pathways for youth to build real skills
How Change Happens

Not a handout. A pathway.

Every InAmigos programme is built to move a person from an urgent need to a lasting capability — in that order, deliberately.

01 — Meet the need

Show up first

A meal, a first-aid kit, a rescued animal, a tree in the ground. The immediate gap gets closed before anything else is asked of anyone.

02 — Build the skill

Teach what lasts

Classrooms, digital training, and livelihood workshops replace one-time relief with a capability someone keeps for life.

03 — Sustain the dignity

Hand over the reins

Interns become staff, trainees become earners, communities become self-sufficient — the goal is always to be needed less over time.

Before You Reach Out

Common questions, answered plainly.

Is InAmigos Foundation a registered, tax-exempt organisation?

Yes. InAmigos is a Section 8 non-profit, 80G & 12A certified, CSR-1 registered, NITI Aayog listed, ISO 9001:2015 certified, and registered on NGO Darpan — meaning donations can qualify for tax benefits and companies can route CSR funds through us.

Do I need experience to volunteer?

No. Most of our drives — food distribution, teaching support, plantation days — are built for first-time volunteers. Project Vikas internships offer structured onboarding for anyone building professional or digital skills.

How much time do I need to commit?

As much or as little as you can offer. Some volunteers join a single weekend drive; others stay on through Project Vikas and become part of the core team, as 250+ interns already have.

Can my company partner with InAmigos for CSR?

Yes — our CSR-1 registration allows corporates to fund or co-run programmes with us. Write to support@inamigosfoundation.org.in and our team will share partnership options.
